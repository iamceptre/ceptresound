<h2><p>It was an honour to play through the remake of Silent Hill 2 from <a href="https://www.blooberteam.com/">Bloober Team.</a></h2>

<p>The thing that improved the experience massively from the original release for me is the audio. <i>(Sound Designer moment, I know)</i></p>
<p>As much as I love the 2001 release, the diegetic audio there always kept me distracted, especially the footsteps with no variations.<br>
What's interesting is that in the <a href="https://youtu.be/E1VKvED76WQ?si=z3drwabU39kUoa7v&t=1324">Team Silent production videos</a>, you can see there are many variations per step, so I guess it got cut due to memory limitations. [*]</p>
<p>The Remake did a great job at expanding the audio for current technology while staying within the original style of sound, leaving many cues almost untouched, or as a layer - Insane job on that!</p>

<p>I've also recorded James's efforts and breathing, where I wanted to reference the original game that had him way more detached from the world. <i>Nice words for me just being bad at acting.</i>
What I like doing while recording grunts is to get into a physical body state, so I recorded me doing way more pushups than I thought I could.</p>
